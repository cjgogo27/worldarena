## 论文中的完整回应结构建议

### Response to Reviewer Comment

**Reviewer's Concern**: "The rationale for operator choices is not well explained, and it remains unclear whether these operators significantly improve solution quality."

**Our Response**:

We appreciate the reviewer's concern and have conducted a comprehensive ablation study to demonstrate the necessity and significant impact of our operator choices. The results are presented in Figures X1-X9 and summarized in Table Y.

**Key Findings**:

1. **Significant Impact on Solution Quality** (Figure X1): 
   - The complete algorithm (ALL operators) successfully serves all 20 requests
   - Removing key operators degrades performance by 70%, serving only 6 requests
   - This demonstrates a 233% improvement attributable to operator diversity

2. **Continuous Improvement Capability** (Figure X2):
   - Effective operators enable solution improvement over 95-162 iterations
   - Single operators fail immediately (0 iterations), indicating inability to escape local optima
   - This validates the iterative refinement rationale of ALNS

3. **Operator-Specific Contributions**:
   - **Historical Removal**: Critical for maintaining solution quality (20/20 requests, 149 iterations)
   - **Random Removal**: Essential for diversification (20/20 requests, 95 iterations)
   - Other operators provide complementary search behaviors when combined

4. **Quality-Efficiency Trade-off** (Figure X5, X8):
   - Additional computation time (367-511s) yields practical, high-quality solutions
   - Fast but low-quality solutions (11s, 6/20 requests) have limited practical value
   - This trade-off is acceptable for real-world applications

5. **Intermodal Capability** (Figure X7):
   - Operator diversity enables true multimodal optimization
   - Single operators fail to utilize all transportation modes effectively

**Rationale for Operator Selection**:

- **Regret-k Insertion**: Prevents greedy myopia by considering k-best insertion positions
- **Historical Removal**: Leverages search history to identify and modify problematic solution components
- **Random operators**: Provide diversification to escape local optima

The ablation study empirically validates that each operator contributes essential search behaviors, and their combination significantly improves solution quality beyond what any single operator can achieve.
